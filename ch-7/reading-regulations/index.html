Chapter 7 · Educational

How to Read a Regulation

A regulation is where a statute’s command gets specific. You read it much like a statute — but you start one step earlier, with the authority that lets the agency speak at all, and you finish by asking whether the codified text is really the whole story. These are the same ADA Title III rules from the last chapter, now in the agency’s hands.

How to move: click any underlined part of the regulation to learn what it is — the panel on the right explains it. The dark buttons navigate the CFR: open the Part’s table of contents and drill into it the same way you navigated the Code. The Source Note opens a comparison of the CFR and the Federal Register.

How to engage with a regulation — the five moves

  1. Start with the authority noteThe statute that lets the agency speak — no authority, no rule.
  2. Read the operative text like a statuteVerbs, exceptions, defined terms — the same precision applies.
  3. Check the Part’s definitionsRegulatory terms are fixed at the Part level, not section by section.
  4. Follow the source note to the Federal RegisterThe preamble is the agency’s explanation — skipping it is the most common regulatory-research mistake.
  5. Confirm currencyA codified rule can be amended, stayed, or vacated after the CFR volume printed.

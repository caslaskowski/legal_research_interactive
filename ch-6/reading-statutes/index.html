Chapter 6 · Educational

How to Read a Statute

A statute states the rule without explaining it — every word carries weight, and the answer to your question is rarely on the page you landed on. So you name the parts, open the table of contents, check the definitions, and let what’s undefined or ambiguous point you to the next source.

How to move: on the statute, click any underlined part to learn what it is and how to read it — the panel on the right explains it. The dark buttons navigate the source: open the table of contents, the definitions, or the Notes of Decision, just as you would in an annotated code.

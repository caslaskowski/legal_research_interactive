Chapter 6 · Practice

Statutes Practice

Three things a statute researcher does constantly: find the answer inside a long section, read what a statute’s status means for the work, and reach for the finding aid that fits what they know.

Nothing here is scored or saved. Pick what you think, then read why — the reasoning for every option is shown, not just the one you chose.

Part 1

Find it in the table of contents

You’re working with the (fictional) Coastal Access Act. You don’t need to read all of it — you need to know which part to open for the question you’re holding. Here is the section’s table of contents.

Coastal Access Act — contents of the section
  • (a)Scope & Applicability — who and what the Act covers
  • (b)Definitions — how key terms are used
  • (c)General Rule — the core duty
  • (d)Specific Obligations — what a covered party must actually do
  • (e)Exceptions — who or what is excused
  • (f)Enforcement & Penalties — consequences of a violation
  • (g)Effective Date — when the rule takes effect

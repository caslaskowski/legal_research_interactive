Chapter 4 · Educational

The Types of Secondary Sources

Secondary sources are your map of the legal landscape. They don’t decide your case, but they orient you, explain a doctrine, and hand you citations to the primary authority that does. The skill is knowing which one to reach for — and that depends on the question you’re holding.

How to use this: click any source to read what it does. Then use the Sort by menu to regroup the list by the question each answers, by how its coverage runs, and by how much persuasive weight it carries. Finish with the two-part check at the bottom.

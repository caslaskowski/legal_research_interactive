Chapter 8 · Educational

Following a Research Hypothesis

A good researcher rarely wanders. You form a hypothesis — a tentative answer — and then test it against the sources in turn, letting each one point to the next. Here you do exactly that for a real veterans-benefits problem, moving from a secondary source down to the case that hands your client an argument.

The client & the hypothesis

A veteran filed for disability compensation and submitted a private physician’s statement. The VA found it “inadequate for rating purposes.” The client disagrees and wants to push back.

Hypothesis: the law entitles the veteran to compensation for a service-connected disability, obligates the VA to help develop the claim — including obtaining an adequate examination — and the courts have set a standard for what makes a medical opinion adequate. If the physician’s statement meets that standard, or the VA skipped its duty, there is room to push back. Let’s test it.

Chapter 4 · Educational

How to Use a Secondary Source

You don’t read a secondary source cover to cover. You read it to answer the question in front of you — and you treat its footnotes as the most valuable thing on the page, because they’re your bridge to the primary authority.

  1. 1Navigate by the table of contents or index, never page one. Jump to the section your question lives in.
  2. 2Stop the moment your question is answered. You’re mining, not reading.
  3. 3The footnotes are the point. Each citation is a candidate answer to your next question — the bridge from where you are to the primary authority.
  4. 4Know when to leave. The moment the source points you to the controlling case or statute, your question has become a focusing question — follow the citation out.

In 2000, the Seventh and Ninth Circuits answered the golf-cart question in opposite ways. What turned that conflict into settled law?

A colleague drafts a brief citing Gil v. Winn-Dixie, 993 F.3d 1266 (11th Cir. 2021), for the rule that websites are not places of public accommodation. Your research-grounded response?

The statute says “place of public accommodation”; the regulation defines it through “facility.” For the website question, why does that pairing matter so much?

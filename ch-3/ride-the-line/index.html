The Research Subway Map

What this module teaches: how to stay on one chain of questions to its end, what to do when a source answers a question from a different chain, and why breaking that discipline is where cognitive overload comes from. The mental model is a subway map: each chain is a colored line, each question–answer pair a station, and the dangerous moment is the interchange — the station where two lines cross and one source serves both.

How to use this: ride the map station by station using the panel on the right — the train moves as you answer. At the interchange you’ll face a real choice; either answer teaches. Transfers you note become tickets in the Research Log, and tickets get spent.
Demonstration — fictional cases & jurisdiction
Duty Standard Knowledge Interchange ▐ terminus

Before boarding

Your client tripped on an obstruction in the shared courtyard path of her apartment complex. Three chains of questions have formed:

Duty Line — what must a landlord do about spaces outside the leased unit?
Standard Line — what makes a condition “unsafe”?
Knowledge Line — must the landlord have known?

Research Log — Notes

    Empty for now. Transfers you note will appear here as tickets.

    The subway rules

    What you just practiced, as rules you can carry.

    Rule 1Ride your line to the endA thread is closed when its last question is answered — not when a shinier question appears.
    Rule 2Interchanges are gifts — if you log themA source that answers another line’s question costs ten seconds to note and saves you a station later.
    Rule 3Never chase mid-lineExiting early leaves open questions behind you and opens new ones ahead — that pile of half-ridden lines is what cognitive overload is.
    Rule 4Teleport only at a terminusWhen a line closes, jump to the next line’s first station and ride it fully — even where it crosses old track.
    Rule 5The log is your transfer ticketThe Notes field is external memory. What’s written down doesn’t have to be held in your head.

    Two quick checks

    Mid-chain on the Duty Line, a case in your results squarely answers the Knowledge Line’s notice question. The method’s move?

    Why insist on riding a line all the way to its terminus before switching?

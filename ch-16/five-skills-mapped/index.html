Five Skills, Mapped

The NextGen Bar Exam tests legal research in Foundational Skills Group C — and its competencies consolidate into five skills you have already been practicing all book long. The exam, in effect, hands you a closed universe of sources and asks you to run a compressed Question Method cycle over them. Nothing here is exam-prep bolted on; it is the method, named for the exam.

How to use this: read the five skill cards, then name the skill being exercised in each of the five tasks below. The practice module hands you an actual closed universe.

The five skills

The NCBE competencies in Foundational Skills Group C consolidate into five skills — and every one is something this book already made you practice.

Skill 1 · Ambiguity spotting

Reading a legal text and identifying terms, phrases, or provisions whose meaning requires interpretive research — undefined terms, vague standards, terms of art, structural ambiguities, scope questions.

Where you built it: Chapter 6 and Appendix C; applied in Chapters 5, 7, and 8.

Skill 2 · Intra-jurisdiction source ranking

Given authorities from a single jurisdiction, ranking them correctly by binding effect — a supreme court over a court of appeals; a statute over common law on the same subject.

Where you built it: Chapter 1; applied throughout.

Skill 3 · Dispositive fact identification

Reading an opinion to identify the facts that, if changed, would change the outcome — then comparing those facts to a client’s facts. The reasoning section is where this skill lives.

Where you built it: Chapter 5 and Appendix B.

Skill 4 · Research gap identification

Recognizing when a source set does not fully answer the question and articulating what would fill the gap — distinguishing “the law is genuinely unclear” from “I have not yet found the answer.”

Where you built it: Chapter 8 — the line Chapters 8 and 9 drilled.

Skill 5 · Research sufficiency and closure

Determining when research is complete — a professional judgment: all questions answered or identified as open, all authorities validated, the framework covered.

Where you built it: Chapter 9; revisited in Chapter 14.
